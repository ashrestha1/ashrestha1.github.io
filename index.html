Designing Customized Online Communication Application and Experiences

This project aims to extend on pre-exisiting online communication applications through focusing directly on the needs of the hearing-impaired.

The goal is to apply the concept of design process introduced in the lectures and gain hands-on experience through this project.

Our learning and execution process

Empathize

Brainstorm

We explored prominent communication applications and tried to identify gaps in the market.

After the first round of brainstorming, we concluded that we as a group wanted to shift our attention towards users that struggle with technology in general.

Our brainstorming resulted in senior citizens as our potential target users but found out that an alarming percent of seniors suffered from some degree of hearing loss. We were concerned by the fact that there was lack of support for the deaf from communication applications, hence we ended up with shifting our target user to deaf people.

Interpret

Constructed POV

Point-Of-View plays a critical part in understanding our target users. To try and empathize with our target users we conducted a mini-experiment. We decided to mute a live lecture video and attempt to follow the lesson, however we were unable to comprehend the slides shown and lose attention in a matter of minutes.

From this experiment we deduced the needs of our target users, which will be utilised to aid our design process.

Ideate

We produced 5 ideas, however after evaluating all of them, we concluded that developing a desktop communicaton application would be the optimal choice.

IOS/Android application

Would not be ideal for users that rely on sign language to convey their message as they would be limited to using only one hand to communicate as the other is needed to hold the phone.

Plugin

Realistically would be impractical as plugins, although are easier to develop, would be difficult to get an agreement with existing comunication applications.

Leap motion

Using leap motion is a great idea as it uses finger and hand movement as input without any physical contact, nonetheless would require users to purchase one, which is relatively expensive compared to a camera.

Gloves

Studies have shown to prove that gloves that track hand movement would be efficent and accurate, yet this is still not optimal as users have to purhcase gloves and would be uncomfortable.

DesktopApp w/ camera tracking handmotion

We decided to design a desktop app that can access the users camera to track handmotion and through deep learning be able to deduce the message.

Verify

Reflection

From this project I gained valuable first-hand experience designing, that I would not be able to get from lectures alone. I learned the importance and procedures of the prototyping approach, and the importance of researching the needs before designing the actual project. Despite the corona pandemic I actually enjoyed the VR room interactions we went through in group meetings, as we were able to convey our thoughts easily through the tools given. I really value the concept of accepting feedback before proceding with updating the project.
